## Pre-Qualification

### Which Solutions Partner designation do we need?
**Solutions Partner for Infrastructure (Azure)** must be active in Partner Center. Other designations (Data & AI, Digital & App Innovation, Modern Work, Security) do not by themselves qualify.

### Were 3rd-party certifications removed?
Yes. Module B Control 1.1 — the 3rd-party certifications requirement — was **removed in V1.6 (Dec 2024)**. Skilling prerequisites surfaced in Partner Center remain in force, however. Always check Partner Center on the day you apply.

### What about the 5 native Azure networking services — does it have to be 5 different customers?
No. The control requires at least 5 services to be demonstrated across **one or more** customer projects within the last 18 months. You can show three customers covering 5+ services collectively.

## Audit Process

### Who conducts the audit?
**Information Security Systems International, LLC (ISSI)** — independent third-party audit firm contracted by Microsoft.

### How long is the audit itself?
- **Module B** alone: 4 hours
- **Module A + B** combined: 8 hours

### How long is a Pass valid?
**Two (2) years.** Module A also satisfies subsequent Azure specialization audits if you remain within the 2-year window (V2.0+ checklist).

### What happens if we fail?
You move into the **Gap Review** phase. ISSI sends a Gap Report within 2 business days; you acknowledge within 2 business days; you complete a Gap Review Meeting (≤ 1 hour) within 15 calendar days, presenting evidence for the Open Action Items. If still not satisfied at the meeting, you get a No Pass and must reapply.

### What format does ISSI want evidence in?
Source documents and live demonstrations. **PowerPoints are accepted for the high-level overview only** — the auditor will then ask to see the source file, repo, pipeline, signed PDF, Microsoft Learn transcript, or live Azure portal view. Expect probing questions.

### Can we anonymise customer names?
Yes for internal docs (Customer A / B / C). The auditor may verify against Partner Center data and signed SOWs — work with your PDM to align.

### Is the audit in English?
Default: English. Some ISSI auditors offer interviews in other languages — ask during scheduling.

## Module A specifics

### Can a Module A Pass from another Azure specialization be reused?
Yes. A previous audit Pass result on V2.0 or later within the last 2 years (or Expert MSP V1.9+) satisfies Module A.

### Does A.3.1 require all three IaC tools?
**No.** As of the Dec 1 2025 refresh, the partner may use **Bicep, Terraform, OR ARM** (any one) — not all three. The **Azure Landing Zone Accelerator** (including Bicep, Terraform, or Azure-portal options) is accepted.

### What can we use for the A.3.3 CI/CD security artifact?
Any **one** of: a security scan report (static analysis or secret scanning), a monitoring dashboard export (Azure Monitor or Log Analytics), an audit-ready YAML pipeline file with integrated security tasks + code-scanning logs (CodeQL or push protection), a Software Bill of Materials (SBOM), or a policy-as-code compliance snapshot (Azure Policy results). All must be PII-free, IP-free, timestamped, and traceable to specific pipeline runs.

## Module B specifics

### How many networking services must we demonstrate?
**At least 5** from: VNet+Peering, ExpressRoute, VPN, Virtual WAN, CDN, Front Door, Load Balancer, Private Link, Traffic Manager, Application Gateway, Azure Firewall. (Control B.3.2.)

### Which scenarios qualify for B.2.1 / B.3.1?
Any one of: secure network infrastructure, on-prem & branch connectivity, remote work at scale, secure global app delivery, 5G & edge computing.

### Does a PoC or pilot count as a completed project?
**No** — unless explicitly permitted by this checklist and supported by documented completion + customer sign-off. Production go-live is the bar.

### What about Cloud Migration Factory engagements?
Yes — added in V1.6 (June 2025). They qualify as evidence as long as they demonstrate the controls.

### What's required for the Well-Architected Review under B.2.2?
**Exported results** from the Microsoft Well-Architected Review (Networking workload picker), covering **2 pillars per customer**, for at least **1 Networking workload** within the last 12 months. Reviews may be conducted before, during, or after deployment.

### What must we show for B.4.1 sign-off?
Documentation of testing, validation, and performance evaluations for 1 customer (within the last 18 months) that meets customer expectations **and includes a customer sign-off**. Verbal sign-off is insufficient.

### What if we operate the customer's network ourselves?
For B.4.2 (Post-deployment Documentation), if you are managing the service on the customer's behalf, your **operation runbooks** are acceptable as alternative evidence.

## Post-Specialization

### How long is the specialization valid?
**Two (2) years** after a Pass. The audit-checklist version active on the day of your audit is what you are audited against, not the version on the day you apply.

### Where does the badge appear?
On your **Microsoft AppSource partner profile**, in **Microsoft Partner Finder**, and downloadable from Partner Center for marketing use.
