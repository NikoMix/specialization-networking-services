## What the Auditor Checks

The auditor verifies you have a **strategy for planning and tracking the completion of cloud & AI adoption projects**. Strategic inputs may include priorities related to analytics, application modernization, or AI adoption.

**Typical questions:**
- How do you build a customer's adoption plan?
- Show me the cost-management report and pricing-calculator output for each customer.
- Show me the DevOps capability assessment outputs.

| 4 | **DevOps Capability Assessment report** — Customer B | PDF | ⬜ |

| 6 | Adoption-planning artifact (plan / workbook / report) — Customer B | PDF, Word, Excel | ⬜ |

> Both customer projects must have completed within the **past 12 months**. At least **two (2) of the listed output documents** are required per customer; cost-management report and DevOps capability assessment are both required.

<Aside type="tip">
  The pricing-calculator output is part of the cost-management report deliverable — capture the calculator share-link as a PDF for evidence durability.
</Aside>

---

## Evidence Guidance

### Cost management report
Use Azure Cost Management + the [Azure Pricing Calculator](https://azure.microsoft.com/pricing/calculator/). Export both to PDF as part of the planning deliverable.

### DevOps Capability Assessment
Run the DevOps Capability Assessment (or a similar maturity assessment your firm uses) and document scoring + recommended uplift actions.

### Planning artifact
Combine into a single per-customer plan covering scope, sequencing, milestones, owners, dependencies, and the cost + DevOps inputs above.

## Common Gaps

- **Pricing-calculator output missing from the cost report:** Always include or link the calculator share-link PDF.
- **Substituting an Azure Migrate assessment for a DevOps capability assessment:** They cover different ground; both may be needed.
- **Only one customer:** The control requires **two unique** customers.
