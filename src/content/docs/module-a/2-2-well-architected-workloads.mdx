## What the Auditor Checks

The auditor verifies you design workloads that are well-architected and **prepared for production**, using Architecture Center reference architectures and a completed **Well-Architected Review** (Go-Live WAR or Standard WAR) for workloads **about to be deployed**.

**Typical questions:**
- Which reference architecture from Architecture Center did you align this design to?
- Show me the exported Well-Architected Review for each customer.
- Where did the WAR change your design before production?

> Two (2) unique customer projects completed within the **past 12 months**.

<Aside type="tip">
  For networking-led engagements the WAR's **Reliability**, **Security**, and **Performance Efficiency** pillars are the most relevant — partners typically include at least these three.
</Aside>

## Evidence Guidance

### Architecture Center alignment
Reference the specific page(s) from [Azure Architecture Center](https://learn.microsoft.com/azure/architecture/) you aligned to. Capture deltas (where the customer's design diverged and why).

### Well-Architected Review
Run the [Well-Architected Review](https://learn.microsoft.com/assessments/azure-architecture-review/) per workload. Export the customer-named report.

## Common Gaps

- **WAR run after go-live:** Control specifically says **before deployment**. If you only have post-go-live WARs, pick a customer where you can re-run before the next workload deploys.
- **Generic "we use the WAR" claim, no exports:** The exported PDF, customer-named, is the evidence.
- **All five pillars chosen but none completed:** Better to do **two pillars deeply** than five superficially.
