## When to use this
Run after the qualification questionnaire is complete. The workshop produces the assessment artifact (B.1.1) and seeds the solution design (B.2.1).

## Inputs you need from the customer

| Input | Why |
|---|---|
| Completed [qualification questionnaire](../qualification-questionnaire/) | Pre-read |
| Current-state topology diagram | Day 1 anchor |
| Workload inventory (top 10 by criticality) | Sequencing |
| Security architect, network architect, app lead | Required attendees |

## Step-by-step

### Day 1 — Current state + topology whiteboard

<Steps>
1. Recap of the qualification questionnaire (30 min)
2. Customer presents current-state topology (60 min)
3. Whiteboard walk: hub(s), spokes, ER / VPN, DNS authority, internet edge (90 min)
4. Gap identification against [CAF network topology](https://learn.microsoft.com/azure/cloud-adoption-framework/ready/azure-best-practices/define-an-azure-network-topology) (60 min)
5. Day 1 readout (30 min): top 5 risks, top 5 opportunities
</Steps>

### Day 2 — Target state options

<Steps>
1. Reference-architecture menu — [hub-spoke vs VWAN vs secure-edge vs multi-region](../reference-architectures/) (60 min)
2. Working session: hub-spoke or VWAN? — decision criteria walk-through (90 min)
3. Working session: Firewall Premium vs 3rd-party NVA — TLS inspection, IDPS (60 min)
4. Working session: Front Door vs App Gateway only vs both (60 min)
5. Decision log seeded — minimum 10 decisions captured with owner + date (30 min)
</Steps>

### Day 3 — Prioritisation + roadmap

<Steps>
1. Backlog generation: from gaps + decisions → epic-level workstreams (60 min)
2. T-shirt sizing per workstream (60 min)
3. Sequencing — quick wins, foundational, transformational (60 min)
4. Roadmap on a page (90 min)
5. Workshop close: signed-off roadmap, named owners, next-step gates (30 min)
</Steps>

## Facilitator notes

- **Mandatory:** capture a **traffic-flow diagram** for at least three representative workloads. This becomes the design's validation reference.
- Bring **2 facilitators** — one drives, one captures.
- Use **Miro / Visio templates** for whiteboards (link your firm's templates here).
- Keep a running **assumptions log** alongside the decision log — assumptions become design risks.

<Aside type="tip">
  If a decision can't be closed in the room, mark it as `Decision pending — owner + date` in the decision log. Do not let it block the design phase — every customer engagement has 2–3 open decisions at design start.
</Aside>
