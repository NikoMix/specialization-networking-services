## How to use
Hypercare is the **bridge between delivery and BAU**. Standard duration: 30 days; extendable in 14-day increments by mutual agreement.

---

# \{Customer} — Hypercare Plan

**Document owner:** \{Author} · **Version:** 0.1 · **Date:** \{YYYY-MM-DD}

## 1. Hypercare window
- **Start date:** \{Go-live + 1 day}
- **Initial duration:** 30 calendar days
- **Extension policy:** 14-day increments by mutual written agreement

Coverage: business hours by default; SEV-1 24/7.

## 3. Severity definitions

| Severity | Definition | Response | Restoration target |
|---|---|---|---|
| SEV-1 | Production down / no traffic | 15 min | 4 h |
| SEV-2 | Significant degradation / partial outage | 30 min | 8 h |
| SEV-3 | Minor issue / workaround exists | 4 h | 2 business days |
| SEV-4 | Question / cosmetic | 1 business day | n/a |

## 4. Defect classification

| Class | Definition | Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| A | Design defect — fix during hypercare at delivery cost |
| B | Configuration drift — fix during hypercare at delivery cost |
| C | Customer-induced change — billable change request |
| D | Enhancement — backlog for next engagement |

## 5. Daily / weekly cadence
- **Daily standup:** 15 min — open incidents, change asks, blockers
- **Weekly steering:** 30 min — defect register, exit-criteria status, lessons

## 6. Exit criteria
All of:
- [ ] Zero open SEV-1 in last 7 days
- [ ] Zero open SEV-2 in last 7 days
- [ ] All Class-A defects resolved
- [ ] Runbook validated by customer team in live execution ≥ 2 times
- [ ] KT sign-off complete (see [KT plan](../kt-plan-template/))
- [ ] Customer signs hypercare-end acceptance

## 8. Post-hypercare
- Defects (Class A/B) carried over land in the warranty period
- Open enhancements transition to the next engagement backlog
- Lesson-learned issues raised in the innersource repo
