## What the Auditor Checks

The auditor verifies you provide **post-deployment operational documentation** showing customers successfully using the new service on Azure:

- Demonstrate how documents, decisions, architectural designs, and procedures were implemented, highlighting any differences from initial design decisions
- Demonstrate **standard operating procedures** for the business-as-usual operations team that describe "how-to" scenarios

**Typical questions:**
- Show me the as-built documentation for this customer.
- Where are the design decisions and deltas from the original HLD captured?
- Walk me through three operational "how-to" SOPs.

## Required Evidence Checklist

| # | Evidence Item | Accepted Formats | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **As-built documentation** for the customer | PDF, Word, Visio | ⬜ |
| 2 | Decision log capturing deltas vs the original HLD | PDF, Word, Excel | ⬜ |
| 3 | Standard operating procedures ("how-to" scenarios) | PDF, Word | ⬜ |
| 4 | *(Alternative if partner is managing the service)* operations **runbooks** | PDF, Word | ⬜ |

> One (1) customer, completed in the last **18 months**. If the partner is managing the service on the customer's behalf, **operation runbooks** are acceptable as alternative evidence to the above.

## Evidence Guidance

### As-built
Generate from the final deployed state (Azure portal exports, IaC parameter files, network topology export). Cross-reference the design decision log.

### SOPs
Cover at minimum: failover (ER ↔ VPN), firewall rule change, certificate rotation (App Gateway / Front Door), DDoS incident response, NSG flow log triage, Connection Monitor alert response.

## Common Gaps

- **As-built identical to the HLD:** As-built should capture **deltas** that emerged during deployment — drift from the design is normal and must be captured.
- **No operational SOPs:** A design doc is not an operational doc — write the BAU "how-to" procedures.
- **Customer engagement &gt; 18 months old:** Pick a fresher engagement.
