## What the Auditor Checks

The auditor verifies you validate the deployment, including:

- A documented process and approach to testing and evaluating performance of network-services deployment
- Assurance the customer's performance and security requirements are met
- Assurance the deployment adheres to Azure best practices
- Remediation for issues revealed during testing

**Typical questions:**
- Show me the test plan and the executed results for this customer.
- Where is the customer sign-off?
- What was remediated as a result of testing, and how?

|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Documented testing & validation process | PDF, Word | ⬜ |

| 4 | Best-practice adherence review | PDF, Word | ⬜ |
| 5 | Remediation log for issues found in testing | PDF, Excel | ⬜ |
| 6 | **Customer sign-off** confirming the solution meets expectations | PDF (signed) | ⬜ |

> One (1) customer, project completed within the last **18 months**. **Verbal sign-off is insufficient** — must be documented.

<Aside type="caution">
  This is the only Module B control that explicitly **requires customer sign-off**. Plan the sign-off step into your engagement schedule — chasing it after the fact is the most common audit blocker on this control.
</Aside>

## Evidence Guidance

### Test plan & results
Use the [Runbook template](../../engagement/deliverables/runbook-template/) `Testing` section as the starting frame. Combine functional tests, performance tests (throughput, latency budgets), and security tests (segmentation, ingress / egress).

### Customer sign-off
A signed PDF (or signed acceptance form in your engagement tooling) explicitly stating the deployment meets customer expectations. Include the customer signatory name, role, and date.

## Common Gaps

- **No sign-off:** The #1 reason this control fails. Build sign-off into the engagement plan from day one.
- **Testing did not cover security requirements:** Add an explicit security-test section (segmentation, NSG enforcement, Firewall policy).
- **No remediation log:** Even a "no defects" log entry with sign-off beats silence.
