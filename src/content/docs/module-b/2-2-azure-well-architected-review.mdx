## What the Auditor Checks

The auditor verifies you use the **Azure Well-Architected Review** for Networking Services workloads, with **two (2) pillars per customer** output. Reviews may be conducted **before, during, or after** deployment.

**Typical questions:**
- Show me the exported WAR for the networking workload, customer-named.
- Which two pillars did you review and why?
- What actions did the WAR drive into the design?

---

## Required Evidence Checklist

| # | Evidence Item | Accepted Formats | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **WAR export** for a Networking workload — customer-named | PDF | ⬜ |
| 2 | **Two pillars** completed in the export | PDF | ⬜ |
| 3 | Action plan derived from the WAR | PDF, Word | ⬜ |

> Minimum: **one (1) Networking workload**, completed within the last **12 months**.

<Aside type="tip">
  For networking the most informative pillars are **Reliability**, **Security**, and **Performance Efficiency**. Pick the two most relevant to that customer's workload risk.
</Aside>

---

## Evidence Guidance

### Run the WAR
Use the [Microsoft Well-Architected Review](https://learn.microsoft.com/assessments/azure-architecture-review/) with the **Networking** workload picker. Complete two pillars in depth.

### Action plan
A short follow-up document that captures the recommendations the WAR generated and which ones are accepted into the design / backlog.

## Common Gaps

- **WAR completed but not exported:** The export PDF is the evidence — re-run and export if the prior run wasn't saved.
- **Only one pillar:** Two pillars per customer is the minimum.
- **No customer name on the export:** Re-run; the export must carry the customer name.
